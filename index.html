Premiere of a defiantly beautiful performance from director Jean Portier

20.02.2020
Author of the article:
Kristina Petrova
Photographer:
Pierre Boucher

The leader of young French directing, creator and director of the independent theater Emigre, released the premiere at the Heberto theater on February 12. Jean Portier invites the viewer to physically feel the passage of time and play with cultural codes: to plunge into the mysterious atmosphere of the early XIX century and solve a mystical, but at the same time romantic riddle. "Last year in Montpellier" is a defiantly beautiful, elegant performance. The production is very different from Portier's other minimalist works. Here You can see a completely different atmosphere, refined and expensive. A two-hour performance based on an ancient mystical story, where truth cannot be distinguished from fiction.

In an expensive hotel, a man assures a woman that they have already met a year ago in Montpellier. The woman believes that this did not happen, or she simply does not want to remember it. Another character is the woman's husband or companion, a gray-haired, grown man of fifty, rich, silent and, probably, making her beautiful idle life possible. The relationships between the three are not defined, cause-and-effect relationships are broken, and the complexity of the plot at first seems absurd. Everything emphasizes how strange this world is, allowing for an infinite number of interpretations, including interpretation through the myth of Orpheus and Eurydice, the idea of the hotel as a purgatory.

Jean Portier offers two options for interacting with the performance: „Or the viewer will try to reconstruct a certain scheme, the most linear of all available to him, build connections, try to understand logically the construction of the plot and the behavior of the characters - and then he will find this production difficult, if not completely incomprehensible, absurd. Or, on the contrary, he will allow himself to be carried away by unusual images that appear before him through the power of the actors’ voices, various noises, music, rhythm or passion of the characters. To such a viewer the performance will seem the easiest of all seen, addressed only to his feelings — ability to see, hear, sence, feel and worry.“.
